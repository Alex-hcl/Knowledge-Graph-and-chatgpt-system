INTTRODUCTION

In the contemporary information-driven world, intelligent systems and AI-powered models have revolutionized the way we access knowledge. These systems, such as ChatGPT, have emerged as crucial tools for facilitating human-computer interactions, information retrieval, and decision-making. However, despite their remarkable capabilities, human interaction with these intelligent systems faces a critical limitation rooted in the cognitive and linguistic abilities of users.

This limitation pertains to the phenomenon of "Partial Knowledge Retrieval". Users often grapple with formulating questions that comprehensively encapsulate their genuine knowledge needs. Concurrently, intelligent systems predominantly respond to the explicit questions posed by users, frequently failing to offer a comprehensive overview of the knowledge domain. This results in a gap in users' understanding of the entire knowledge landscape.

Problem Indentification

This research focuses on the challenge of interactions with Chatgpt. Users' limited expression and system responses confined to explicit questions hinder effective knowledge acquisition. While previous research has explored system capabilities, there's a gap in understanding how to help users better comprehend knowledge and ask improved questions. Additionally, some ChatGPT responses are overly verbose, potentially causing user impatience.
